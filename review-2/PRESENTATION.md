# Review 2 Presentation Outline

## Slide 1: Title

- A Mobile Application for Real-Time Spatial Territory Capture and Social Fitness
- Student name
- Guide name
- Department / College

## Slide 2: Problem Statement

- Existing fitness apps track activity but lack real-time spatial competition
- Location-based games are engaging but not fitness-focused
- Need a system that combines exercise, competition, and social interaction

## Slide 3: Proposed Solution

- GPS-based mobile application for walking and running
- Real-world map divided into virtual tiles
- Users capture territory by moving physically through regions

## Slide 4: Objectives

- Track user movement in real time
- Capture and update tile ownership
- Provide leaderboards and challenges
- Support social interaction and nearby user visibility
- Reduce spoofing using server-side validation

## Slide 5: Literature Review

- Fitness apps focus on pace, route, and distance
- Location-based games focus on map engagement and competition
- Gamification improves user motivation and retention
- Social reinforcement increases continued participation

## Slide 6: Gap Identification

- No strong integration of fitness tracking with live territory capture
- Limited support for persistent spatial ownership in fitness apps
- Existing solutions do not fully combine social fitness and geospatial competition

## Slide 7: System Architecture

- Mobile client

- Leaderboard and social modules
