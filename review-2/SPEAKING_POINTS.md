# Review 2 Speaking Points

## Slide 1

This project is titled A Mobile Application for Real-Time Spatial Territory Capture and Social Fitness. The aim is to combine walking and running activity with map-based competition and social interaction.

## Slide 2

Existing fitness applications mainly focus on metrics such as pace, distance, and route history. However, they do not create a persistent competitive map experience. On the other hand, location-based games are engaging but are not built around structured fitness improvement.

## Slide 3

Our solution is a GPS-based mobile application that divides the real-world map into virtual tiles. As users move through physical locations, they capture territory, compete with others, and improve their fitness activity at the same time.

## Slide 4

The main objectives are real-time tracking, tile capture, leaderboards, social interaction, challenge systems, and server-side movement validation to reduce spoofing.

## Slide 5

The literature review shows that fitness apps are effective for tracking, location-based games are effective for engagement, and gamification improves retention. This project combines those strengths into a single system.

## Slide 6

The gap is that current systems do not strongly integrate fitness tracking, spatial ownership, social competition, and persistence together in one mobile platform.

## Slide 7

The architecture uses a mobile client and a backend server. The mobile app handles UI, GPS tracking, and session display. The backend handles validation, persistence, territory ownership, and leaderboard-related state.

## Slide 8

The development followed an iterative prototype methodology. We first designed the mobile app, then implemented tracking and map logic, and then added backend persistence and authentication support.

## Slide 9

The main modules are activity tracking, territory capture, map visualization, social interaction, rewards and leaderboards, authentication, and movement validation.

## Slide 10

The implemented prototype includes a mobile app with Home, Track, Map, Nearby, and Profile screens, GPS tracking, tile capture logic, a backend API, and local data persistence.

## Slide 11

The analysis shows that the concept is technically feasible. The prototype successfully demonstrates that movement can be converted into territory ownership and that social fitness engagement can be layered on top.

## Slide 12

The current limitations are that the backend is local, cloud deployment is pending, and advanced multi-user real-time features are not fully completed yet.

## Slide 13

Future work includes cloud hosting, full multiplayer sync, notifications, advanced reward systems, and production release workflows.

## Slide 14

In conclusion, the system provides a new way to combine fitness, competition, and social interaction in a location-based mobile application.
